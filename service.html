Our Solution

Our Bacteriophage Assisted Medicine Transportation Solution

Step 1: Targeted Detection

Bacteriophages, minute viruses, are introduced into the bloodstream. These specialized agents seek out smooth muscle cells, honing in on areas of concern such as vessel tears and plaque formation.

Step 2: Precise Intervention

Upon locating plaque, bacteriophages inject cyclodextrins directly into the fibrous cap of the plaque. Cyclodextrins act as molecular sponges, absorbing excess cholesterol within the plaque structure.

Step 3: Natural Resolution

With the excess cholesterol absorbed, foam cells within the plaque transition into macrophages. Macrophages, the body's natural scavengers, begin to digest the cholesterol-laden plaque. This enzymatic breakdown leads to a reduction in plaque size, mitigating the risk of heart attacks associated with coronary artery disease (CAD).
